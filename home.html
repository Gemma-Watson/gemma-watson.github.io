What I Teach

Curriculum Areas

I have planned, delivered and evaluated lessons and units across Stages 4, 5 and 6 in Technology and Applied Studies.

Computing Technology

Students explore simulations, ethical technology use, and real-world digital applications. Lessons link directly to emerging technologies and industry relevance.

Stage 5 focus: digital systems, simulations, and programming fundamentals.

Textiles and Design

Students learn design principles, garment construction, and sustainable production. They develop creative solutions through visual research and folio documentation.

Stage 6 focus: elements and principles of design, function, and aesthetics.

Food Technology

Students investigate how culture, society and technology influence food habits. Lessons include practical applications, reflection, and discussion around health and sustainability.

Stage 5 focus: cultural influence on cuisine and food product innovation.

Technology Mandatory

Students examine sustainability and design thinking through projects such as tiny home design and engineered systems. They explore real-world solutions and collaborative investigation.

Stage 4 focus: design for need, safe work practice, and iterative prototyping.
